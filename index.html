Meet Colleen

A native of Sturgeon Bay, Wisconsin, attended St. Joseph’s Catholic School through 8th grade, and graduated from Sturgeon Bay High School in 2000. At age 16, Colleen spent her junior year of high school in Caracas, Venezuela as a Rotary Youth Exchange Student, where she had the opportunity embrace a new culture and become fluent in Spanish.

Colleen graduated cum laude from Marquette University in 2005 with a Bachelor’s degree in Political Science and a minor in history. She received her J.D. from Marquette University Law School in 2008, along with a graduate certificate in Dispute. As a law student, Colleen was on the editorial staff of “Marquette Elder’s Advisor,” a law journal for which she served as managing editor during her final year. Colleen worked as a law clerk at Willms, S.C., a law firm specializing in tax and estate planning, located in Theinsville, Wisconsin.

In 2008, Colleen was admitted to the State Bar of Wisconsin as well as being admitted to practice in the United States District Court for the Eastern and Western Districts of Wisconsin.

Colleen began her legal career in private practice in 2008 at Brabazon Law Office, located in Green Bay, Wisconsin. In 2010, Colleen partnered with her friend and colleague, Megan Carollo, and opened M.C. Law Group, LLC, a law firm located in Green Bay, Wisconsin.

Colleen focuses her practice on criminal defense and family law. She utilizes a unique approach in her practice by incorporating alternative dispute resolution techniques in an attempt to achieve the best possible resolution in every case.

Because there are so few Spanish-speaking attorneys in Northeastern Wisconsin, Colleen practices throughout Northeastern Wisconsin, including Door Co., Brown Co., Kewaunee Co., Calumet Co., Outagamie Co., Manitowoc Co., Oconto Co., Shawano Co., and Oneida Tribal Court.

Colleen’s professional memberships include the State Bar of Wisconsin, the Wisconsin Association of Criminal Defense Attorneys, and the Brown County Bar Association, for which she has served as a committee member since 2014.

She has been actively involved in the Wisconsin High School Mock Trial program, first as a student herself, later as an attorney coach at Green Bay East High School, and most recently as a regional tournament coordinator.
